Smart Adaptive Headlamp Cutoff System

  1. Problem statement:
    High-beam headlights from oncoming vehicles can create glare during night driving, reducing visibility and increasing the risk of accidents. Manual switching between high and low beams may not always be effective, creating the need for an automated solution.
  2. Solution:
    The Smart Adaptive Headlamp Cutoff System uses LDR sensors and an Arduino UNO to automatically detect ambient light conditions and switch between high beam and low beam headlights. This reduces glare for other drivers while maintaining adequate visibility for the vehicle driver.
  3. Components:
    Component Quantity
    Arduino Nano 1
    LDR Sensors 3
    10K Resistors 3
    220K Resistors 2
    LEDS 2
    Breadboard 1
    Jumper Wires As required
  4. Technologies Used:
    • Arduino Nano
    • Embedded C Programming
    • Sensor Based-Automation
    • LDR Technology
    • Electronic circuit design(Tinkercad)
    • Click here to view Circuit diagram
  5. Working:
    The system continuously monitors light intensity using three LDR sensors. When darkness is detected, the high beam is activated to improve visibility. When bright light from an approaching vehicle is detected, the system automatically switches to low beam mode to reduce glare and improve road safety.
  6. Key Features:
    • Automatic Light High beam and Low beam switching
    • Real Time light detection
    • Glare reduction
    • Enhance road safety
    • low Cost Implementation
  7. Application:
    • Samrt Vehicle Lighting System
    • Automatic Safety System
  8. Conclusion:
    The Smart Adaptive Headlamp Cutoff System provides an intelligent and cost-effective solution for vehicle headlight control. By automatically adjusting beam intensity based on surrounding light conditions, the system improves road safety, enhances driving comfort, and reduces the risk of accidents during night driving.
